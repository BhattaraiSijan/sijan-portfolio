Practicing Empathy with the Jobs-to-be-Done Framework

During a course called Innovation and Creativity, I was introduced to the Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) framework, and it completely changed how I view human behavior and product design. This framework argues that people don't simply use products; they "hire" them to get specific jobs done. Understanding this concept reshaped how I think about empathy.

One day, while using the MARTA app (Atlanta's public transit system), I started analyzing every feature through the JTBD lens. What jobs were users hiring the app for? What were their pain points? It fascinated me and made me wish I had documented my observations. Applying JTBD this way helped me understand the frustrations and motivations of commuters on a deeper level.

Over time, I began using JTBD everywhere: observing shoppers at grocery stores, students in classrooms, or families at public parks. By breaking down actions into smaller tasks, I could understand people's needs, emotions, and pain points. This practice has greatly enhanced my empathy and understanding of others.

For businesses, using the JTBD framework to build empathy is a game-changer. It allows companies to design products and experiences that genuinely resonate with users, addressing real jobs they’re trying to accomplish. It’s a way to connect on a human level and create solutions that matter.
